# Strategy: timing-led

**Module:** timing-led
**Hypothesis:** An outreach that opens with a real, sourced timing signal (industry trend, market shift, program or regulatory change) gives a prospect a concrete reason to act *now* — not a reason invented to manufacture urgency.

## Purpose

Lead with a real, verifiable industry or market timing signal that makes now a meaningful moment for the prospect's decision. The signal must be true, sourced, and specific to their industry or situation.

---

## Research brief (extends standard base)

Spawn the standard researcher (`references/research-base.md`) with emphasis on timing signals:

> In addition to the standard brief, focus especially on: (1) any near-term industry-specific trend or pressure (e.g. material cost changes, labor market shifts, regulation effective dates, seasonal capital cycles); (2) any market-wide signal relevant to the type of deal you're facilitating (e.g. rate environment, program changes, market conditions); (3) any sector-specific change (e.g. new program announcements, incentives, regulatory shifts). Return the signal with a source URL and a plain-English one-sentence summary. If no credible signal is found, return "no timing signal identified" — do not invent one.

**Hard rule:** the timing signal must be true and cited. A vague claim ("the market is changing") with no source is not a timing signal — it is filler. If no real signal is found, fall back to `proof-led` for this prospect rather than sending a fabricated timing hook.

---

## Angle selection

Pick the single strongest timing signal the researcher returned. Rank:

1. **Program-specific signal** — most actionable (e.g. a program structure change, new incentive, deadline).
2. **Market/rate-environment signal** — broadly relevant (e.g. rate movement, market conditions).
3. **Industry-specific signal** — most specific to their world (e.g. material costs, regulation, seasonal cycle).

If multiple signals exist, pick the one most relevant to the prospect's likely decision: use industry-specific if it maps directly to their situation; use market-wide if the prospect is rate-sensitive or timing-sensitive.

---

## Draft shape

```
[Timing signal — the gift. One or two sentences, plain language, with source attribution inline or in natural phrasing.]

[One sentence connecting the signal to their specific situation.]

[Standard CTA: one clear next step, low commitment.]

[Easy out.]
```

Thread to any prior context. One product per touch. If a rate or price is referenced, ground it in `config/products.md`.

---

## Guardrails

- **No fabricated signals.** Every timing claim must trace to a verifiable source. Vague claims with no citation are not acceptable.
- **No fabricated rates or terms.** Ground all rate language in `config/products.md`.
- **If no signal found:** fall back to `proof-led` for this prospect. Do not send a timing-led draft without a real signal.
- **No "approved" / "pre-qualified" language.**
- **Cite the signal source** — the sender needs to be able to verify it before sending.
