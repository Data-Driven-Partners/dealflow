# Strategy: product-reframe

**Module:** product-reframe
**Hypothesis:** Many outreach threads die because the wrong product was pitched for where the prospect was at the time. Re-angling to a product they were never shown — or that fits them better now — opens a door the original pitch couldn't.

## Purpose

Identify a product in `config/products.md` that the prospect was NOT introduced to in any prior context, and re-angle the outreach around that product as a fresh option. For new cold contacts with no prior thread, identify the non-obvious product that best fits their current profile.

---

## Research brief (extends standard base)

Before spawning the researcher, **read the prospect's context** (from the source adapter's `context` field): what is their current situation? What product are they most likely to have already been pitched or to associate with your category? Then:

1. **Identify the product gap.** What offering from `config/products.md` was *not* surfaced that might fit them now?
   - Is there a product that fits their current stage better than the default?
   - Is there an entry-point product that's lower friction to start with?
   - Is there a product that fits their current pain point better than the primary offering?

2. **Check the fit criteria** (`config/products.md`) to confirm the alternative product is actually available to a business with their profile. Do not re-angle to a product they don't qualify for.

3. Spawn the standard researcher (`references/research-base.md`) for fresh company context. Emphasis: has anything changed (or is there evidence suggesting) that makes an alternative product more relevant now?

---

## Angle selection

Pick ONE alternative product. Rank by fit to their current situation as described in `config/products.md`. The honest trade-off must be named. One product per touch. Show the math if a number is used (trace to `config/products.md`).

---

## Draft shape

```
[One sentence establishing the new angle — e.g. "I wanted to flag a [product type] that might actually be a better fit for where you are right now, and here's why."]

[1–2 sentences explaining the product and why it fits their situation — grounded in config/products.md and what the researcher found about their current state.]

[Optional: one company-specific hook from the researcher if it strengthens the angle.]

[Standard CTA: one clear next step, low commitment.]

[Easy out.]
```

If there is a prior thread, reference it so the prospect knows this is a continuation, not a fresh cold email.

---

## Guardrails

- **Ground the alternative product in config/products.md.** Never re-angle to a product the prospect can't access.
- **Show the honest trade-off.** Don't oversell; name the trade-off plainly (e.g. "faster to close but higher rate").
- **No fabricated rates or terms.** All numbers from `config/products.md`.
- **Do not re-pitch a product that already failed** if there is a prior thread — the whole point is the angle change.
- **No fabrication in the research hook.** If nothing new is found about the company, omit the company-specific line.
